What Is the Format of the U.S. Citizenship Test?

Last updated July 2026 — based on the USCIS Policy Manual, Volume 12, Part E

The U.S. Naturalization Test is not a written, multiple-choice exam. It's an in-person, oral interview conducted by a USCIS officer.

To pass and become a U.S. citizen, you must pass two main components during your interview: the Civics Test and the English Test.

🏛️ Part 1: The Civics Test

Your civics format depends entirely on when you filed your Form N-400 application. USCIS updated the exam rules for applications submitted on or after October 20, 2025.

Which Test Will You Take?

2008 Version

  • Who takes it: Anyone who filed their N-400 before October 20, 2025.
  • Total question pool: 100 possible questions.
  • Questions asked: Up to 10 oral questions.
  • To pass: 6 answers correct.
  • How it ends: The officer stops asking questions the moment you get 6 right.

2025 Version (New Rules)

  • Who takes it: Anyone who filed their N-400 on or after October 20, 2025.
  • Total question pool: 128 possible questions.
  • Questions asked: Up to 20 oral questions.
  • To pass: 12 answers correct.
  • How it ends: The officer stops the test once you reach 12 correct answers (pass) or 9 incorrect answers (fail).
👶 The “65/20” Special Exemption: If you're 65 years or older and have been a permanent resident (Green Card holder) for at least 20 years, you take a simplified test. You only need to study a specific list of 20 questions, you'll be asked 10, and you only need 6 correct to pass.

🗣️ Part 2: The English Test

The English component is the same for all applicants, regardless of which civics version applies to you. It evaluates three basic skills:

1. Speaking & Understanding

Format: Continuous evaluation throughout the entire interview.
Task: There's no separate “quiz” for this part — the USCIS officer judges your ability to speak and understand English based on your normal conversation and your answers about your Form N-400 application.

2. Reading

Format: Oral reading.
Task: The officer shows you a sentence on a tablet or paper. You have up to 3 attempts to read 1 sentence out loud correctly.

3. Writing

Format: Physical or digital writing.
Task: The officer reads a sentence out loud. You must write the sentence exactly as spoken. You have up to 3 attempts to write 1 sentence correctly.

Want to practice the vocabulary used in the reading and writing tests? Visit English Test Practice.

🔄 What Happens if You Don't Pass?

Don't worry — failing a portion of the test doesn't mean your application is rejected or that you have to pay the fee again.

  • The 2nd chance: USCIS automatically schedules a re-test interview between 60 and 90 days later.
  • Only retake what you missed: You're only re-tested on the exact section you didn't pass. For example, if you passed the English portion but missed the civics test, you'll only retake the civics test on your second visit.

Source: USCIS Policy Manual, Volume 12, Part E, Chapter 2
