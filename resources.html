Official Resources

Last updated July 2026

This site is an independent study tool, not an official source. For anything that matters legally — your eligibility, your filing, your test results — always defer to USCIS directly. Here are the official resources this site draws from, plus a few more that are useful while preparing.

Start Here

  • USCIS Citizenship Resource Center — the main hub for everything naturalization-related.
  • Civics Test Updates — current officeholders for questions whose answers change (President, Vice President, Speaker of the House, etc.).
  • Form N-400, Application for Naturalization — the official application.

Civics Test Study Materials

  • Study for the Test — USCIS's full library of free study materials for both the 2008 and 2025 civics tests.
  • Preparing for the Naturalization Test: A Pocket Study Guide (M-1122) — the official pocket guide, including the English reading and writing vocabulary lists.
  • 2008 Civics Practice Test — USCIS's own online practice tool (English and Spanish).
  • 100 Civics Questions with MP3 Audio — official audio of every 2008-version question and answer.

Other Languages

USCIS publishes many study materials in Spanish, Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean, Tagalog, Arabic, and more. This site currently only has content in English, so if you'd like to study in another language, these official translations are the most reliable option:

  • Find Study Materials and Resources — filter by language on this page for translated guides and flash cards.

The Naturalization Process

  • 10 Steps to Naturalization — the official step-by-step overview (also summarized on our Process page).
  • USCIS Policy Manual, Volume 12, Part E — the authoritative source on English and civics testing rules.
  • A Guide to Naturalization — a full overview of eligibility requirements and what to expect.

Free Help

  • Find Help in Your Community (via the Citizenship Resource Center) — free or low-cost citizenship classes, legal assistance, and USCIS-funded programs near you.
